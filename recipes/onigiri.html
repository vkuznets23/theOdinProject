Onigiri

Ingridients:

  • 2 cups Japanese short-grain rice
  • 2 1/2 cups water
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • Nori (seaweed) sheets, cut into strips (optional)
  • Filling of your choice (e.g., pickled plum, tuna mayo, cooked salmon, or any other filling you like)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Rice
    1. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. This removes excess starch.
    2. Add the rinsed rice and 2 1/2 cups of water to a rice cooker or pot.
    3. Cook the rice according to the rice cooker's instructions or bring the pot to a boil, then cover and reduce the heat to low. Cook for about 15-20 minutes, or until the water is absorbed and the rice is tender.
  2. Season the Rice
    1. In a small bowl, mix the rice vinegar, sugar, and salt until dissolved.
    2. Once the rice is cooked, transfer it to a large bowl. While the rice is still warm, gently fold in the vinegar mixture using a wooden spoon or spatula. Allow the rice to cool slightly until it is comfortable to handle.
  3. Shape the Onigiri
    1. Wet your hands with a mixture of water and a little salt to prevent the rice from sticking.
    2. Take a small amount of rice (about 2 tablespoons) and flatten it slightly in your hand. Place a small amount of your chosen filling in the center.
    3. Mold the rice around the filling into a triangle or oval shape, pressing gently but firmly to compact the rice.
    4. Optionally, wrap the formed onigiri with a strip of nori.
  4. Serve

Onigiri can be eaten warm or at room temperature. They are perfect as a snack, in a bento box, or as a side dish. Enjoy!
